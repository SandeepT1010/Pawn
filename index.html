How the pawn moves

The pawn can move 2 square forward on its first turn, but then can only move one square after.

When a pawn reaches the opposite side of the board then it can promote/turn into another piece except for a king and pawn.

The pawn is useful because it is worth less that other pieces most of the time and can attack them.

The pawn has a special move to capture other enemy pawns called en passant. It is when the enemy pawn moves two square forward and your pawn is next to it, then your pawn can diagnolly move and capture the enemy pawn.

The pawn can capture pieces that are forward 1 square diagonally.

The pawn is worth 1 point.
